East Africa

In 2011, Toby Tanser completed his “Sea to the Stars” run from the Indian Ocean to the top of Kilimanjaro. His run raised over $100,000 for the construction of the Shoe 4 Africa hospital, East Africa's first and only public children’s hospital.

Inspired by Toby, from June to October 2024, I ran over 1000 miles through East Africa and raised over $10000 for Shoe 4 Africa.

My run took me from the Tanzania coast to the top of Kilimanjaro to the Shoe 4 Africa Hospital itself and onwards to Uganda and Rwanda. The adventure also included fastest known times on Mt Meru, the fifth tallest mountain in Africa, and Ol Doinyo Lengai, the "Mountain of God".

Western Canada

From January 1 to May 1, 2024, I set out on a human-powered journey from my front door in Seattle. I ran with a baby jogger holding my camping gear, food, and skis. This adventure was inspired in large part by my friend Matt Montavon who ran 1,100 miles across Alaska with a stroller. The run raised money for GiveDirectly.
