Washington and Canada

From January 1 to May 1, 2024, I set out on a human-powered journey from my front door in Seattle. I ran with a baby jogger holding my camping gear, food, and skis. This adventure was inspired in large part by my friend Matt Montavon who ran 1,100 miles across Alaska with a stroller. The run raised money for GiveDirectly.

Africa

Help build the first children's cancer hopsital in East Africa.

Donate

In 2011, Toby Tanser completed a “Sea to the Stars” run traveling from the Indian Ocean to the top of Kilimanjaro to raise money for the construction of the first children’s hospital in East Africa, which opened its doors in 2015. At the time, Tanser lived in New York City where he was head coach of the NYC Nike Running Club and served on the board of directors for the NYC Marathon for 15 years.

Now, almost ten years after the hospital opened, Toby's charity Shoe 4 Africa is building another hospital, the first Children's Cancer Hospital in East Africa! In order to raise money for the construction of this hospital (currently 20% complete), I will follow in Toby's footsteps running to Kilimanjaro, and then I will continue running onwards to Eldoret, Kenya where Toby lives today.

My personal motivation to follow in Toby's footsteps comes from understanding his journey, how he carried the weight of such a grand mission to the top of Kilimanjaro, and why he turned his back on New York City— the place I was born. I fly to Africa with my family on June 8 where we will go on a safari. When my family flies home, I will stay and run from the coast to Eldoret.
